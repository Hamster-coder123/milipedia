# Public Perception Research: F-15 Eagle / Strike Eagle

Aircraft: McDonnell Douglas F-15 Eagle, McDonnell Douglas F-15E Strike Eagle, Boeing F-15EX Eagle II

This file is reputation research for the F-15 article. It separates public language from confirmed facts. Fan claims, social-media language, and nickname culture are not used as performance proof unless they are supported by official, museum, safety, or aviation-history sources.

## 1. General Reputation

The F-15 is publicly associated with air superiority, high speed, large radar, strong climb performance, Israeli combat use, Desert Storm, and long service life. Aviation communities commonly treat it as the benchmark fourth-generation air-superiority fighter.

The most common public reputation terms are:

| Term | Meaning | Exact treatment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 104-0 | Shorthand for the F-15 air-to-air record | Use only as a claim about confirmed air-to-air combat losses, not all losses |
| Undefeated Eagle | Fan phrase based on the air-to-air record | Explain that accidents, ground-fire losses, structural failures, and friendly-fire incidents are separate categories |
| Mudhen | F-15E Strike Eagle nickname | Use as unofficial nickname culture |
| Missile truck | F-15EX payload shorthand | Use as public debate language, not an official mission title |
| One-wing Eagle | Reference to the 1 May 1983 Israeli F-15D landing | State the exact event instead of treating the phrase literally |

## 2. What the F-15 Is Known For

The F-15 is known for:

- F-15A first flight on 27 July 1972.
- First Eagle delivery in November 1974.
- First combat-squadron delivery in January 1976.
- Israeli first combat victories on 27 June 1979.
- F-15C and F-15D entry into USAF inventory in 1979.
- Bekaa Valley / Lebanon air combat in 1982.
- Israeli one-wing landing on 1 May 1983.
- F-15E initial operating capability in September 1989.
- Desert Storm air-superiority role and F-15E Scud-hunting missions.
- F-15E GBU-10 kill against an Iraqi Mi-24 on 14 February 1991.
- Black Hawk fratricide on 14 April 1994.
- Missouri F-15C longeron failure and breakup on 2 November 2007.
- F-15EX acceptance on 10 March 2021.
- F-15EX Eagle II naming on 7 April 2021.
- F-15EX full-rate production approval in June 2024.

## 3. Public Debates

| Debate | Public claim | Exact article treatment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Air-to-air record | The F-15 is undefeated | State that no confirmed F-15 air-to-air combat loss is established in the common record; do not imply no F-15 has ever been lost |
| F-15EX | The Eagle II replaces stealth fighters | State that F-15EX complements fifth-generation aircraft with payload, range, open architecture, and capacity; it is not a low-observable aircraft |
| One-wing landing | The F-15 can fly without a wing | State that an Israeli F-15D landed after losing most of one wing in a specific emergency |
| F-15 versus F-16 | One is always better | Treat the comparison as mission, cost, payload, range, and force-structure dependent |
| F-15 versus F-22/F-35 | The F-15 is obsolete because it is not stealthy | State that lack of stealth is a real limitation but not proof of zero usefulness |
| F-15 versus Su-27 | Western heavyweight versus Soviet heavyweight | Treat as a doctrine and era comparison; avoid unsourced one-plane-wins claims |

## 4. Incidents That Shape Reputation

| Date | Event | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 27 June 1979 | Israeli F-15s achieved the type's first combat victories | Establishes that the first combat reputation came from Israel |
| June 1982 | Israeli F-15s fought in the Lebanon / Bekaa Valley air battles | Builds the Eagle's early combat reputation |
| 1 May 1983 | Israeli F-15D landed after losing most of one wing | Central public toughness legend |
| 17-20 January 1991 | USAF lost two F-15E Strike Eagles during Desert Storm to ground defenses according to aviation-history summaries | Corrects misuse of the air-to-air record as an all-losses claim |
| 14 February 1991 | F-15E crew destroyed an Iraqi Mi-24 with a GBU-10 | Unusual air-to-air story associated with the Strike Eagle |
| 14 April 1994 | Two USAF F-15s shot down two U.S. Army UH-60 Black Hawks, killing 26 people | Necessary counterweight to celebratory F-15 reputation |
| 2 November 2007 | Missouri Air National Guard F-15C broke apart after upper right longeron failure | Shows structural-aging and manufacturing-conformance risk |
| June 2024 | F-15EX full-rate production approval | Shows the Eagle family remained in active procurement after five decades |

## 5. Balanced Summary

The F-15 is remembered as the heavyweight benchmark of fourth-generation air superiority: large, fast, heavily armed, combat-proven, and surrounded by a public mythology that is mostly rooted in real events but often overstated online. The article should praise exact achievements while also covering friendly-fire, ground-loss, accident, aging, and non-stealth limitations.
